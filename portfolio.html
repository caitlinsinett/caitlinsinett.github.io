Completing the 2,854 miles of the Tour Divide, a grueling father-son adventure

After a long journey through the United States, I interviewed Peter Kraft Sr., a Gainesville resident who had completed a 2,854-mile bike race with his son. This article was very interesting to me because I met Kraft Sr. in person, and I could see how this journey had affected him physically. Late-blooming lawyer now holds prestigious Bar position

Eugene K. Pettis is a University of Florida graduate who became the first black president of The Florida Bar in 2013. Pettis discussed his family life while growing up, and he talked to me about his journey up to becoming The Bar's president. I enjoyed this feature story because it was fascinating to me how this man had gone from wanting to be a dentist to being the 65th president of The Florida Bar. Here's the link.

Feds now back morning-after pills for all girls

This Gainesville Sun article was about how a U.S. district judge ruled that girls of any age would be able to buy emergency contraception without a prescription. In Gainesville, a Publix pharmacist said the morning-after pill was not purchased very often, but when I talked to a Walgreens pharmacist, she said the exact opposite. She said the pill was purchased almost two or three times a day. The story was a challenge because not many pharmacists from stores wanted to talk to me, so I had to call many different stores and speak to managers in order to get to the pharmacists that would talk to me. I also had less than 10 hours to write this article for The Gainesville Sun. Here's the link.

WUFT News

Florida National Lyme Disease Symptoms Misdiagnosed At Alarming Rate

I wrote this article for WUFT News, and it was about people who had been bitten by ticks and did not know it. These people had Lyme disease and had symptoms ranging from brain fog to joint pain. I spoke with University of Florida's Dr. Holly Donohoe who told me the number of people who reported having Lyme disease in the U.S. has increased over the past decade. She explained that the main problem is physicians don't know how to diagnose the disease. This story was extremely interesting to me, and I was curious to know how Lyme disease had affected these people. Here's the link.
